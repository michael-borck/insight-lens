# Exploring Trends

Trends tell you whether a unit is getting better, worse, or staying flat. InsightLens surfaces trends in three places.

## Dashboard trend chart

The **Overall Experience Trend** card on the [Dashboard](../getting-started/dashboard-overview.md) plots the average overall-experience percentage across every semester you have data for. It's a single line averaged across all units — useful for spotting institution-wide shifts.

- Hover any point to see the exact value and semester label.
- If you only have one semester imported, the chart stays empty until you import more.

## Unit detail trend

For a deeper look, open any unit from the **Units** list or from a dashboard card. The [Unit Detail](unit-detail.md) page shows a per-unit trend line with one point per offering, so you can see semester-over-semester movement for that unit alone.

## Top Performers and Units Needing Attention

Both of these dashboard cards (and their full-page equivalents under [Performance Reports](../reports/performance-reports.md)) are trend-aware. "Needing attention" isn't just a low score — it factors in whether the score is declining across recent offerings. That's what makes them more useful than a flat sorted list.

## Tips for reading trends

- **Two points is not a trend.** If a unit has only been offered twice, treat any change with caution.
- **Watch the response rate.** A unit that gained 10 points in overall experience but also dropped from 60 % to 15 % response rate is not a success story — the smaller sample is just louder.
- **Compare like offerings.** Semester 1 and Semester 2 cohorts are often different. Where possible, compare Semester 1 to Semester 1.
